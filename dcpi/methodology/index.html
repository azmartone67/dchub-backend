DCPI Methodology v2

The Data Center Power Index is a transparent, reproducible scoring framework for global data center markets. This document describes the scoring components, weights, data sources, refresh cadence, and known limitations.

Coverage

DCPI v2 scores 132 markets across 12+ countries. Markets are defined as metropolitan areas with ≥3 verified data center facilities. The market universe is updated weekly from DC Hub's facility database (21,000+ verified records).

Scoring Components

40%Grid Headroom

Available power capacity per market relative to current draw. Calculated from EIA RTO sub-balancing-authority data (60+ zones across 7 US ISOs), national TSO data for international markets, and SEC EIA-861 capacity filings.

grid_headroom_score = 100 × (1 - current_demand / nameplate_capacity) × congestion_factor

25%Pipeline Velocity

Pace of new construction relative to existing operational capacity. Counts facilities in permitting, construction, and planned status. Higher score = faster pipeline growth.

pipeline_velocity = (pipeline_mw / operational_mw) × normalization_factor

20%Energy Cost Efficiency

Inverse of average $/kWh in the market. Lower energy costs = higher score. Sourced from EIA retail electricity rates by state (US) and equivalent national datasets internationally.

energy_efficiency = 100 × (max_market_kwh - market_kwh) / (max_market_kwh - min_market_kwh)

15%Facility Density

Number of operational facilities per market. Proxies for ecosystem maturity, fiber/peering availability, and competitive supply.

density_score = log10(facility_count + 1) × 25

Refresh Cadence

Scores recompute daily at 06:00 UTC. Raw component data refreshes per its source cadence (above). Historical scores are preserved for trend analysis (90-day rolling window publicly, full history with Enterprise plan).

Limitations

DCPI is a relative ranking, not an absolute measure of market quality. International markets currently use proxy data for energy pricing (full $/kWh requires Pro+ in-region partners). Grid headroom for non-US zones uses national TSO aggregates, not sub-regional LMP. Pipeline data depends on public permit/announcement disclosure; private builds may be undercounted.
