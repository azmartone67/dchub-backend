## Slide 6 — Revenue Architecture

A three-phase commercial path designed to land Phase 1 in weeks, not quarters.

### Phase 1 — Data Citation Partnership
- **Cost to CBRE:** $0
- **What CBRE gives:** Data byline on H2 2026 ("Data: DC Hub") + intro to CBRE Research lead
- **What DC Hub gives:** Live data feed + Research desk Q&A access + permission to cite "CBRE H2 2026 data partner" on dchub.cloud
- **Why this lands first:** Procurement-free. Pat's discretion alone. Closes in weeks.

### Phase 2 — Revenue Share (Co-Branded Subscription)
- **Cost to CBRE:** $0 upfront — built on existing CBRE Research subscription infrastructure
- **Pricing:** Set by CBRE inside CBRE Insights tier ($20–50K/seat/year typical CBRE range)
- **Revenue split:** DC Hub receives **20% of net new subscription revenue** attributable to the DC Hub-powered tier
- **Attribution:** Mutually agreed tracking parameters; quarterly reconciliation; 45-day payout
- **Why this works:** CBRE owns the customer relationship and pricing power; DC Hub provides the live engine that justifies the premium tier

### Phase 3 — Enterprise Broker License
- **For:** CBRE Data Center Solutions team (~30 specialists globally)
- **Includes:** Full DC Hub Land + Power map, 50+ infrastructure layers, site scoring, MCP/API access
- **Price:** $10–25K per broker per year (volume-tiered)
- **Annual envelope:** $150K – $750K range depending on seat count
- **Why this works:** Tools that directly contribute to winning $M+ brokerage mandates pay back immediately
