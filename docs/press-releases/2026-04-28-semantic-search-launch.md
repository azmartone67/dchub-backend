SAN FRANCISCO, April 28, 2026 — DC Hub, the data center intelligence platform tracking 20,000+ facilities across 140+ countries, today launched semantic search powered by Cloudflare Vectorize. For the first time, operators, developers, and investors can query the platform in natural language and filter results by ISO/grid territory (PJM, ERCOT, CAISO, MISO, SPP, NYISO, ISO-NE, NWPP), capacity range, status, and provider — all returning in under 200 milliseconds at the edge.

The release includes a free public explorer at **dchub.cloud/api/v1/explorer** and a native MCP integration that surfaces 25+ DC Hub tools to Claude Desktop, ChatGPT Connector, and Cursor users. Developers can hit the API directly at **dchub.cloud/api/v1/search/semantic** or use natural language through any MCP-compatible client.

"DC databases have been SQL-shaped for 20 years," said Jonathan, founder of DC Hub. "An operator looking for 30 MW with PJM access shouldn't have to write a join. We just made the entire industry queryable in English."

**Pricing:**
- Free tier — 10 calls/day, 5 results/call, public explorer
- Developer — $49/mo, 1,000 calls/day, full grid filters
- Pro — $199/mo, 10,000 calls/day, semantic search + Neon hydration + rerank
- Enterprise — $699/mo, 100,000 calls/day, AI agents, grid intelligence

The platform also shipped grid-aware embedding upgrades, hybrid score reranking (composite of vector similarity × power capacity × status weight), and a sub-50ms edge fast-path for high-traffic queries.

**About DC Hub** — DC Hub is the data center intelligence platform powering site selection, M&A diligence, and capacity planning for hyperscalers, REITs, and investors. dchub.cloud.
