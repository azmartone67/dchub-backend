// Competitor Profiles

What Each Platform Actually Does

Each competitor serves a narrow slice of what DC Hub delivers end-to-end. Understand the gaps before the conversation.

Baxtel · Est. 2015
Colocation Directory with a Map

Founded by a colocation industry veteran, Baxtel is a well-maintained buyer's directory. It lists ~4,400 US facilities with specs and routes procurement inquiries. It does not track energy infrastructure, M&A, capacity pipelines, or development activity.

  • No energy or power infrastructure data
  • US-centric; global coverage thin
  • No API, no real-time refresh, no M&A layer
  • Buyer-matching model, not market intelligence
datacenterHawk · Leasing Intel
Real Estate Absorption Analytics

datacenterHawk is a solid product for CRE professionals focused on leasing — quarterly absorption reports, vacancy trends, and pricing across ~105 markets. Excellent for brokers and developers; thin for investors, operators, and energy professionals.

  • No energy or power infrastructure layer
  • No M&A transaction database
  • Leasing-centric; no site selection tooling
  • Enterprise pricing; no API access
DatacenterDynamics (DCD)
Media Brand Entering Intelligence

DCD is the industry's largest publication — conferences, magazine, news, and training. Their DCD>Intelligence platform launched in 2025, built on 25 years of editorial relationships. Behavioral and intent data from readership; not an operational data platform.

  • Intelligence product newly launched in 2025
  • Content/media model, not data infrastructure
  • No energy mapping or M&A tracking
  • No API; engagement-oriented
Data Center Frontier (DCF)
Editorial & Industry News

DCF is a respected trade publication — strong editorial, news coverage, and event presence. Not a data or analytics platform. Valuable for awareness and market narrative but offers no queryable intelligence, no facility database, and no infrastructure mapping.

  • Purely editorial — zero structured data product
  • No facility database or coverage tracking
  • No M&A, energy, or pipeline analytics
  • Content platform only
Datacenters.com
Procurement Marketplace & RFP Tool

Datacenters.com connects buyers to colocation providers via an RFP/quoting marketplace. Excellent for enterprises shopping for colo space. Oriented entirely to procurement — not market intelligence, investment analysis, or infrastructure planning.

  • Demand-side procurement only
  • No developer or investor intelligence layer
  • No energy, M&A, or pipeline data
  • No API or programmatic access
CBRE & JLL · Advisory Giants
CRE Advisory with Periodic Reports

CBRE and JLL are the preeminent commercial real estate advisors. Their semi-annual North American reports are widely cited, authoritative, and useful for macro positioning. But data access requires an advisory engagement — you get a PDF, not a platform.

  • Semi-annual snapshots, not live intelligence
  • No energy infrastructure or M&A database
  • Advisory fees required; no self-serve access
  • CRE-focused; no developer/operator tooling
