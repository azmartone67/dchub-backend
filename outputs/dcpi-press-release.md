# DC Hub Launches DCPI — First Daily-Updated Power Availability Index for U.S. Data Center Markets

**Phoenix, AZ — May 9, 2026** — DC Hub today announced the launch of the **Data Center Power Index (DCPI)**, the first daily-updated, publicly-cited scoring system for power availability across U.S. data center markets.

DCPI scores every U.S. market on two axes:
- **Constraint Score** (0–100, high = avoid): queue wait time, reserve margin proximity to NERC floor, demand-growth YoY, recent grid emergency frequency.
- **Excess Power Score** (0–100, high = opportunity): the contrarian metric. Combines reserve-margin headroom, queued generation additions, renewable curtailment volume, queue approval rate, stranded interconnection at retiring plants, and behind-the-meter industrial generation.

The Excess Power Score surfaces capacity hidden in plain sight — markets where buyers don't know to look. Today's top three BUILD-verdict markets, with under twelve months to power:

- **Cheyenne, WY** — Excess Power 69.5 / Constraint 22.5
- **Rural SPP, KS** — Excess Power 67.2 / Constraint 22.5
- **Williston, ND** — Excess Power 65.0 / Constraint 17.4

The bottom of the Constraint list reads as expected: Northern Virginia (60-month queue, 12.0% reserve margin), Phoenix (42-month queue, 13.5% reserve), Atlanta (36-month queue, 14.0% reserve).

"Site selection consultants charge $100K and 6–8 weeks for what DCPI delivers in 1.4 seconds," said Jonathan Martone, founder of DC Hub. "The data isn't proprietary — it's public ISO filings and generation pipeline data. We just made the math public, daily, and citable."

DCPI is freely available at **dchub.cloud/dcpi**. The full methodology and machine-readable JSON are at **dchub.cloud/api/v1/dcpi/scores**. Historical CSV downloads are at **dchub.cloud/data/dcpi-history.csv**. Per-market research reports are auto-generated at **dchub.cloud/research/<market>**.

DCPI updates daily at 06:00 UTC. The full index, including AI chat, market deep-dives, and a 30-day chart of top BUILD market trajectories, is on the dashboard.
